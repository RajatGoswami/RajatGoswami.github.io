Computer Science

I enrolled in the Computer Science program at the University of Waterloo because I believed a problem-solving mindset and the technical ability to build on my ideas would serve me well in whichever industry I ended up in. That belief holds true today. What I didn't expect was the anxiety and isolation associated with an environment that places too much value on academic performance and employment status. Similary, the technology industry too often overlooks the unforeseen consequences of their products in favour of user growth and profit. I'd like my work as a software developer to support the argument that, despite these pitfalls, technology can be a force for good.


Business Administration & Finance

Access to quality education, a better standard of living and a general sense of belonging in modern society is heavily dependant on how much money we have access to. Business school provided me with a basic understanding of how wealth is collected, grown and transferred but falls short in educating students on how inequally it's often distributed. I hope to provide people with the resources to achieve a well-rounded understanding of finance and the skills to exercise more control over their economic well-being.
